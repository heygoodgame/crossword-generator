# Crossword Construction Design Principles

Below are core guiding principles for how we think about attempting to create mini and midi crossword puzzles.

* * *

## Mini Crosswords

**Grid and Structure**

-   5×5 grid Sunday through Friday, expanding to 7×7 on Saturdays
-   The 5×5 grid is the defining visual signature — instantly recognizable and unchanging six days a week
-   Typically 5 Across and 5 Down clues on weekdays (10 total), with more on Saturday's 7×7
-   Word lengths on weekdays range from 3 to 5 letters; on Saturday from 3 to 7 letters
-   A small number of black squares (typically 2–5) appear in the 5×5 grid to delineate word boundaries; it is not a fully dense grid
-   Every white cell is "checked" — each letter participates in both an Across and a Down answer, consistent with American crossword conventions
-   The grid does not strictly require 180-degree rotational symmetry the way the full crossword does, but patterns tend to be clean and visually balanced
-   On rare occasions (like Earth Day 2022), the black-square pattern can form a recognizable picture (e.g., a redwood tree), but this is the exception rather than the rule
-   No rebus squares, no colored squares, no visual effects — it is a pure, traditional crossword grid

**Themes and Content**

-   The 5×5 weekday puzzles are essentially unthemed — the grid is simply too small to support a traditional crossword theme with a revealer
-   The Saturday 7×7 occasionally incorporates light thematic connections or a unifying concept, but this is not guaranteed
-   Special dates (holidays, cultural moments) may inspire creative grid designs, but day-to-day the puzzle is about individual clue quality, not thematic coherence
-   There is no revealer clue

**Difficulty and Progression**

-   Monday is generally the most approachable; Saturday is the trickiest and leans more on wordplay
-   That said, the difficulty curve is much gentler than the full crossword — the challenge level is broadly consistent throughout the week
-   Saturday feels harder partly because of the larger 7×7 grid and partly because of more clever misdirection in clues
-   The puzzle is designed to be solvable in under 5 minutes; experienced solvers often finish weekday puzzles in under a minute
-   The Saturday 7×7 noticeably increases solve time due to 12–26 additional letter cells

**Clue Style**

-   Clues are playful, punny, and contemporary — designed for a broad, general audience
-   Pop culture references are common and skew modern: TV shows, movies, musicians, internet culture, sports figures
-   Question marks at the end of clues signal wordplay or puns rather than literal definitions
-   Fill-in-the-blank clues appear regularly
-   Cross-reference clues ("With 8-Across..." or "See 5-Across") are used, especially on Saturdays where there's room for multi-entry phrases
-   Abbreviation indicators ("Abbr.") follow standard crossword conventions
-   The tone is more casual and accessible than the full daily crossword — it's an entry point for new solvers

**Fill and Vocabulary**

-   Common crossword short-fill staples ("crosswordese") appear — words like ERA, ORE, INK, SKA, IBM — but Mini Crosswords keep these to a minimum
-   With only 10 entries on a weekday, every answer needs to pull its weight; there's almost no room for throwaway "glue" fill
-   Proper nouns, brand names, abbreviations, and slang are all fair game
-   The vocabulary skews toward recognizable, everyday English rather than obscure or archaic words
-   Three-letter answers are common and often the easiest footholds for solvers

* * *

## Midi Crosswords

**Grid and Structure**

-   Typically a 9×9 grid, but can stretch to 10×10 or 11×11 depending on the theme — the grid size is intentionally variable
-   The variable sizing is a defining characteristic: unlike Mini Crosswords' rigid 5×5/7×7 structure, Midi Crosswords flexes to serve whatever the theme requires
-   Occasionally features unusually shaped grids when the theme calls for it
-   Word lengths range from 2 letters up to 9, 10, or even 11 letters on longer themed entries
-   Roughly 11–15 Across clues and a similar number of Down clues per puzzle (varies with grid size)
-   Black squares are present and follow conventional crossword placement, but the patterns may be more creative or irregular than the full crossword
-   All white cells appear to be fully checked, following standard American crossword convention

**Themes and Content**

-   Every Midi Crossword has a theme — this is a firm editorial commitment, not optional
-   Themes are creative and often conceptual, not just "three long answers that share a trait"
-   Theme concepts in the first 11 days included a GUMBALL MACHINE (with 34 ORB shapes depicted in the grid) and a VELVET ROPE (with colored squares representing the rope and the answer POST appearing twice for the two posts)
-   Revealer-style clues are present (e.g., "Barrier to entry for some, as seen in this puzzle's colored squares" for VELVET ROPE)
-   The themes are more ambitious and playful than what you'd typically see in a Monday or Tuesday full crossword, despite the smaller grid
-   Cross-reference clues linking thematic entries together appear regularly

**Relaxed Construction Rules**

-   Two-letter words are explicitly permitted — a significant departure from the full crossword's strict three-letter minimum
-   Repeating answers are allowed when they serve the theme (e.g., POST appearing twice in the Velvet Rope puzzle)
-   These relaxed rules give constructors more creative freedom to execute unusual theme concepts that wouldn't be possible under traditional construction guidelines
-   The editorial posture is deliberately more experimental than the full daily crossword

**Digital-First Visual Effects**

-   About once a week, puzzles might include a visual effect — an animation, colorful shading, or interactive flourish
-   These effects can appear when you start the puzzle or after you solve it
-   Colored squares can be integral to the theme (e.g., colored squares representing a velvet rope, or shapes representing gumballs)
-   The visual effects are not decorative — they are thematically integrated, reinforcing the puzzle's concept
-   This is the single most distinctive attribute that separates Midi Crosswords from others

**Difficulty and Solve Experience**

-   Difficulty is approachable — roughly Monday to Tuesday level on the full crossword's scale
-   One reviewer solved a Midi Crossword in just over 3 minutes, suggesting a target solve time of 3–7 minutes for most solvers
-   The puzzle is positioned as "more than Mini Crosswords but less than daily crosswords" in both time commitment and mental challenge
-   Cluing is clever but not obscure; the puzzle rewards broad general knowledge rather than deep crossword expertise
-   The thematic ambition is more reminiscent of a Wednesday or Thursday full crossword, even though the difficulty is easier

**Clue Style**

-   Clues are contemporary, playful, and accessible — similar in register to Mini Crosswords rather than the Friday/Saturday daily crosswords
-   Modern slang is embraced (SESH, NOOBS, SELFOWN)
-   Pop culture spans a wide range: Metallica, S.N.L., Coca-Cola, nursery rhymes, NFL players, video game terminology
-   Wordplay and misdirection are present but not at the level of a late-week daily crossword
-   Fill-in-the-blank and abbreviation clues follow standard conventions

**Identity and Positioning**

-   Explicitly positioned as a "bridge" puzzle — for solvers who find Mini Crosswords too quick and daily crosswords too daunting
-   "builds on one's crossword streak," "a perfect place to grow your crossword habit"
-   The variable grid size, visual effects, relaxed construction rules, and mandatory themes collectively make it feel like a different species of puzzle from Mini Crosswords, despite occupying a similar time-commitment niche
